THE PROBLEM(S)

1
When it comes to the environment, people think they’re already doing everything they can.
2
There are a lot of guilty feelings associated with the environment due to “virtue-signaling.”
3
Users are much more motivated to make behavioral environmental changes when there is a personal benefit, or when it is convenient.

“I don’t want to sound cynical, but there’s nothing we can do to change the world’s trajectory.”

“I wouldn’t recycle our cardboard if we didn’t have a cardboard recycling thing.”

“People take the moral high-ground and push it in your face how environmentally friendly they are.”

SURVEY DATA

*Worldwide, the livestock industry produces just as much greenhouse gas as the transportation industry.

THE CHALLENGE

Create a website that is informational without being boring, and compelling without eliciting guilt—all while convincing users that their individual efforts can make a difference.

DESIGN PROCESS

We got acquainted with our user

We did our research and math homework

And came up with this:

A main page that responds to the user’s selection of how much meat they were willing to cut back on

Testing Results

+

—

  • Users liked the fact that the information on the page adjusted depending on how much they were willing to commit
  • Some users were confused by the copy—does the slider indicate the days you are eating meat or the days you’re not?
  • Users were surprised at how much of an impact they could make by cutting back on meat
  • Many users did not understand that the slider was clickable
  • Users generally felt that the copy was empathetic—reportedly more motivating than guild-driven messaging

We made some changes, and tested some more

CONCLUSION

This project changed the way I personally view my own eating habits, and my own personal responsibility I have for the planet that I live on and love. For the entirety of the month following the completion of this project, my roommates joined me in going a month without eating meat.

A project like “Every meal matters” deserves and requires a robust marketing campaign—the effectiveness of the site grows with participation from site users.

There are a few additional features that we would like to add based on user feedback:

70% of our survey responders reported that they would be willing to spend more to buy sustainably sourced meat—and yet, many don’t. We would like to add a page that locates local meat distributors for users.

A lot of testers were especially enthused about how much many they could save by cutting back on meat. That insight gave us the idea to have a feature where users could donate the money that they would otherwise be spending on meat. As part of this feature, we’ve envisioned letting users vote on where we as a community should by land with the amount of money that’s been donated that would otherwise be used for the livestock industry.

This site and a lot of the additional features that we have planned for it would require a lot of back-end development—skills beyond our expertise as designers and front-end developers. Connecting with developers along with additional testing are the next steps in the life of this project.
